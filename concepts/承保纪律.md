# 承保纪律 / Underwriting Discipline

## 概念解析 / Definition

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 承保纪律指保险公司在市场价格不足以覆盖风险时，主动放弃业务、缩小保费规模的自律行为。巴菲特认为这"有悖于一般机构的本能行为"，因为眼睁睁看着竞争对手以愚蠢的价格抢走生意，需要非同寻常的管理纪律。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Underwriting discipline refers to the self-restraint of voluntarily giving up business and shrinking premium volume when market prices are inadequate to cover risks. Buffett considers this "contrary to normal institutional behavior," as watching competitors take away business at foolish prices requires extraordinary managerial discipline.

## 核心要义 / Key Principles

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 保险行业的周期性：费率充足 → 竞争者涌入 → 费率下降 → 承保亏损 → 竞争者退出 → 费率回升
> - 在费率下降阶段，大多数机构会为了维持保费规模而接受不合理的价格
> - 伯克希尔选择主动缩量，等待市场恢复正常
> - 1974-75 年危机后竞争对手退出，国民保险借此获得了量和利润的双重增长
> - 承保纪律的核心悖论：短期看是"损失"，长期看是生存和盈利的关键

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - Insurance industry cyclicality: adequate rates → competitors enter → rates fall → underwriting losses → competitors exit → rates rise
> - During rate decline, most institutions accept inadequate prices to maintain volume
> - Berkshire chooses to shrink voluntarily, waiting for market normalization
> - After the 1974-75 crisis, competitor exits allowed National Indemnity to gain both volume and profit
> - Core paradox: short-term "loss" but long-term key to survival and profitability

## 实践应用 / Practical Application

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 评估保险公司时，关注其在费率下行周期中的行为
> - 能在困难时期坚持承保纪律的公司，长期更值得投资
> - 承保纪律差的公司往往在"好年景"看起来业绩出色，但在周期反转时遭受重创

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - When evaluating insurers, observe their behavior during rate-down cycles
> - Companies that maintain discipline in tough times are better long-term investments
> - Poor discipline often looks great in "good years" but devastates when the cycle turns

## 巴菲特原话精选 / Buffett Quotes

> [!quote] [1977-letter](../letters/1977-letter.md)
> 当市场再度松动、费率变得不足时，我们又将面临一个考验：从心态上坦然接受保费收入的减少。这需要非同寻常的管理纪律，因为眼睁睁看着别人以愚蠢的价格抢走生意，实在有悖于一般机构的本能行为。
>
> As markets loosen and rates become inadequate, we again will face the challenge of philosophically accepting reduced volume. Unusual managerial discipline will be required, as it runs counter to normal institutional behavior to let the other fellow take away business — even at foolish prices.

> [!quote] [1979-letter](../letters/1979-letter.md)
> 我们听过很多保险公司经理人说愿意减少保费规模以换取承保盈利，但真正说到做到的却寥寥无几。Phil 是个例外：合理的保单他就签，不合理的一概拒绝。我们相信，这种坚定意志既罕见又明智——而且是经营一流意外险公司绝对不可或缺的。
>
> We hear a great many insurance managers talk about being willing to reduce volume in order to underwrite profitably, but we find that very few actually do so. Phil Liesche is an exception: if business makes sense, he writes it; if it doesn't, he rejects it. We believe such strong-mindedness is as rare as it is sound — and absolutely essential to the running of a first-class casualty insurance operation.
