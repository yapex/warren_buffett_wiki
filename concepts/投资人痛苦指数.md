# 投资人痛苦指数 / Investor's Misery Index

## 概念解析 / Definition

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> "投资人痛苦指数"是巴菲特在 1979 年股东信中首次提出的概念，定义为通货膨胀率加上股东将公司年度收益转化为个人可支配收入时必须缴纳的税率（股利需缴普通所得税，留存收益需缴资本利得税）。当这个指数超过企业的股本回报率时，投资人的购买力（真正的资本）就会缩水，即便他什么都没有消费。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> The "Investor's Misery Index" is a concept Buffett first introduced in the 1979 letter, defined as the inflation rate plus the percentage of capital that must be paid by the owner to transfer annual earnings into personal spendable income (ordinary income tax on dividends and capital gains tax on retained earnings). When this index exceeds the business's return on equity, the investor's purchasing power (real capital) shrinks even though he consumes nothing at all.

## 核心要义 / Key Principles

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 通胀率 + 税负 = 投资人痛苦指数
> - 痛苦指数 > 股本回报率 → 购买力缩水（即使不消费）
> - 即便伯克希尔维持 20% 的年复合增长，在 14% 通胀率下，税后购买力增长接近于零
> - 剩余的约 6 个百分点大部分会被所得税吃掉
> - 企业对此束手无策——高通胀不会帮助赚取更高的股本回报率
> - 类比：3% 储蓄债券 → 5% 银行存折 → 8% 国库券，一个接一个被通胀从增值工具变成蚕食购买力的工具
> - 1964 年每股账面价值换半盎司黄金，1979 年还是半盎司——政府擅长印钞，但不擅长印黄金

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - Inflation rate + tax burden = Investor's Misery Index
> - Misery Index > ROE → purchasing power shrinks (even without consumption)
> - Even with Berkshire maintaining 20% compounded growth, at 14% inflation, after-tax purchasing power gain approaches zero
> - The remaining ~6 percentage points are largely consumed by income tax
> - Companies have no solution — high inflation won't help earn higher returns on equity
> - Analogy: 3% savings bonds → 5% passbook savings → 8% Treasury notes, each in turn transformed from value-enhancing to value-destroying by inflation
> - Book value per share in 1964 bought half an ounce of gold; in 1979, still half an ounce — government excels at printing money, not gold

## 巴菲特原话精选 / Buffett Quotes

> [!quote] [1979-letter](../letters/1979-letter.md)
> 通货膨胀率加上股东将公司年度收益收入囊中时必须缴纳的资本比例——这两者合起来可以称作"投资人痛苦指数"。当这个指数超过企业的股本回报率时，投资人的购买力（真正的资本）就会缩水，即便他什么都没有消费。对此我们束手无策，因为高通胀率并不会帮我们赚取更高的股本回报率。
>
> That combination — the inflation rate plus the percentage of capital that must be paid by the owner to transfer into his own pocket the annual earnings achieved by the business — can be thought of as an "investor's misery index". When this index exceeds the rate of return earned on equity by the business, the investor's purchasing power (real capital) shrinks even though he consumes nothing at all. We have no corporate solution to this problem; high inflation rates will not help us earn higher rates of return on equity.

> [!quote] [1979-letter](../letters/1979-letter.md)
> 假设我们能继续维持 20% 的年复合增长——这绝非轻而易举或十拿九稳的事——而且这个增长像过去十五年一样完全反映在伯克希尔的股价上，那么在 14% 的通货膨胀率下，各位税后购买力的增长很可能接近于零。
>
> If we should continue to achieve a 20% compounded gain — not an easy or certain result by any means — and this gain is translated into a corresponding increase in the market value of Berkshire Hathaway stock as it has been over the last fifteen years, your after-tax purchasing power gain is likely to be very close to zero at a 14% inflation rate.
