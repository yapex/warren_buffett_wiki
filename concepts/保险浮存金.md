# 保险浮存金 / Insurance Float

## 概念解析 / Definition

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 保险浮存金（Insurance Float）指保险公司先收取保费、后支付理赔的时间差所形成的可用资金。巴菲特称之为"别人的钱"——保险公司可以在不支付任何成本的情况下持有和投资这些资金。如果承保盈亏平衡（综合比率 = 100），浮存金就是免费的；如果承保盈利，浮存金甚至带来负成本。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Insurance float refers to the funds available from the time difference between when premiums are collected and claims are paid. Buffett calls it "other people's money" — insurers can hold and invest these funds at no cost. If underwriting breaks even (combined ratio = 100), float is free; if underwriting is profitable, float even carries a negative cost.

## 核心要义 / Key Principles

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 伯克希尔 1977 年保险投资成本从两年前的 $134.6M 增至 $252.8M，主要由保费增长带来的准备金增加驱动
> - 浮存金规模随保费增长而自动增长，形成"飞轮效应"
> - 再保险业务产生的浮存金占保费收入比例异常之高
> - 浮存金是巴菲特投资帝国的核心"杠杆"——无需借款即可获得大量投资资金

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - Berkshire's insurance investments grew from $134.6M to $252.8M in two years, driven primarily by reserve growth from premium increases
> - Float grows automatically with premium volume, creating a "flywheel effect"
> - Reinsurance generates unusually high float as a percentage of premiums
> - Float is the core "leverage" of Buffett's investment empire — large investment funds without borrowing
