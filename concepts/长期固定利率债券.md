# 长期固定利率债券 / Long-Term Fixed-Rate Bonds

## 概念解析 / Definition

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 长期固定利率债券指期限在 15 年以上、利率固定的债务工具。巴菲特在 1979 年股东信中对此类债券进行了猛烈抨击，认为它是通胀肆虐的世界里仍在定期签订的"最后一类主要的长期固定价格合约"。他指出，保险公司一边认为一年期保单定价期限太长，一边却把保费以固定价格借出去三四十年，这种行为极其荒谬。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Long-term fixed-rate bonds are debt instruments with maturities exceeding 15 years at fixed interest rates. In the 1979 letter, Buffett launched a vigorous attack on such bonds, calling them "the last major fixed price contract of extended duration still regularly initiated in an inflation-ridden world." He pointed out the absurdity of insurance companies deeming one-year policy terms too long while lending premium proceeds at fixed rates for thirty or forty years.

## 核心要义 / Key Principles

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 超长期固定利率债券是通胀时代最后的"长期固定价格合约"
> - 在几乎所有其他商业领域，长期合同要么与价格指数挂钩，要么坚持每年重新审视
> - 债券领域存在"理念上的滞后"——卖方（贷款人）在经济和合约革命中酣然大睡
> - 会计惯例允许保险公司按摊余成本记录债券，无视缩水的市场价值，这反而助长了损失
> - 伯克希尔自身也犯了错：买入 15 年期债券是错误，没有及时卖出更是严重错误
> - "在别人沉睡时半梦半醒并不能充分保护自己"
> - 波洛尼厄斯忠告（改写版）："既不做短期借款人，也不做长期放贷人"
> - 巴菲特预测：买了 2010 或 2020 年到期债券的保险公司可能面临持续不断的大麻烦

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - Very long-term fixed-rate bonds are the last remaining "long-term fixed price contracts" in the inflationary era
> - In virtually all other commercial areas, long-term contracts are either indexed or insist on annual review
> - A "cultural lag" prevails in bonds — sellers (lenders) slept through an economic and contractual revolution
> - Accounting convention allowing insurers to carry bonds at amortized cost, ignoring impaired market values, actually contributed to losses
> - Berkshire itself made mistakes: buying 15-year bonds was wrong; not selling them sooner was a worse mistake
> - "You do not adequately protect yourself by being half awake while others are sleeping"
> - Polonius (slightly restated): "Neither a short-term borrower nor a long-term lender be"
> - Buffett predicted: insurers with 2010 or 2020 maturities may face major and continuing problems

## 实践应用 / Practical Application

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 伯克希尔的债券策略：近几年净投入仅限于可转换债券（转换权使实际期限远短于票面到期日）
> - 这一策略使伯克希尔的债券未实现损失远小于绝大多数财产意外险公司
> - 对股票投资的强烈偏好也帮助了伯克希尔——债券配置总体维持在较低水平
> - 巴菲特承认在债券方面栽了跟头，并认为自己的错误比那些毫不在意的同行更不可原谅

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - Berkshire's bond strategy: net commitments limited to convertible bonds in recent years (conversion options effectively shorten the average life)
> - This policy produced significantly lower unrealized losses than most P&C insurers
> - Strong preference for equities also helped by keeping overall bond allocation low
> - Buffett acknowledged mistakes in bonds, considering them less excusable than those of competitors who were oblivious to developing problems

## 巴菲特原话精选 / Buffett Quotes

> [!quote] [1979-letter](../letters/1979-letter.md)
> 许多人一边认为在通胀环境下一年的保单定价期限都太长了，转过身来就把卖掉半年期保单收到的保费，以固定价格借出去三四十年。
>
> Having decided that one year is too long a period for which to set a fixed price for insurance in an inflationary world, they then have turned around, taken the proceeds from the sale of that six-month policy, and sold the money at a fixed price for thirty or forty years.

> [!quote] [1979-letter](../letters/1979-letter.md)
> 在别人沉睡时半梦半醒并不能充分保护自己。买入十五年期的债券是一个错误，而我们偏偏就犯了；更严重的错误是，当我们现在的观点开始成形时，我们没有（即便是亏本也要）卖掉它们。
>
> You do not adequately protect yourself by being half awake while others are sleeping. It was a mistake to buy fifteen-year bonds, and yet we did; we made an even more serious mistake in not selling them (at losses, if necessary) when our present views began to crystallize.

> [!quote] [1979-letter](../letters/1979-letter.md)
> 就像我们不愿意现在就为 2010 年或 2020 年交付的一磅喜诗糖果或一码伯克希尔布料定一个固定价格一样，我们同样不愿意买入那些为未来数十年的资金使用权定下固定价格的债券。总的来说，我们赞同波洛尼厄斯的忠告（略作改动）："既不做短期借款人，也不做长期放贷人。"
>
> Our unwillingness to fix a price now for a pound of See's candy or a yard of Berkshire cloth to be delivered in 2010 or 2020 makes us equally unwilling to buy bonds which set a price on money now for use in those years. Overall, we opt for Polonius (slightly restated): "Neither a short-term borrower nor a long-term lender be."
