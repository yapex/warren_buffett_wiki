# 1981 年致股东信摘要 / 1981 Letter to Shareholders — Summary

## 概述 / Overview

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 1981年，巴菲特在致股东信中延续了对经济现实的深刻剖析。这一年，伯克希尔的经营收益为3,970万美元，相当于期初股本的15.2%，低于1980年的17.8%。巴菲特重点阐述了三个核心议题：无控制权持股的收益概念、股权投资的附加价值问题，以及保险行业的严峻挑战。此外，巴菲特还宣布了创新的"股东指定捐赠"计划，受到股东的热烈响应。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> In 1981, Buffett continued his profound analysis of economic reality. Operating earnings were $39.7 million, representing 15.2% of beginning equity capital, down from 17.8% in 1980. The letter focused on three core topics: the concept of non-controlled ownership earnings, the issue of equity value-added, and the severe challenges facing the insurance industry. Additionally, Buffett announced the innovative "shareholder designated contributions" program, which received enthusiastic shareholder response.

## 关键主题 / Key Themes

### 无控制权持股收益 / Non-Controlled Ownership Earnings

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特重申了"未分配收益"的重要性概念。伯克希尔在那些既不控制也不具有重大影响力的公司中拥有的股份，这些公司的未分配收益虽然未被计入经营报表，但终将转化为股东的实质价值。巴菲特指出，这种转化在时间上会极不规律，但从长期来看是确定无疑的。1982年，仅在盖可保险、通用食品、雷诺烟草和华盛顿邮报这四家公司，伯克希尔应占的未分配收益预计就超过3,500万美元。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett reiterated the importance of "undistributed earnings." Berkshire's stakes in companies it does not control or significantly influence have undistributed earnings not included in operating statements, but these will eventually translate into tangible value for shareholders. Buffett noted this translation would be irregular in timing but certain over the long term. In 1982 alone, Berkshire's share of undistributed earnings from just four companies—GEICO, General Foods, R.J. Reynolds, and Washington Post—was projected to exceed $35 million.

### 股权投资的附加价值 / Equity Value-Added

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 这是1981年信中最具洞察力的部分。巴菲特指出，在高利率环境下（1981年长期国债收益率超过16%），典型的美国企业（股本回报率约14%）对个人投资者来说在经济上已是"差"企业：
> - 如果企业将所有收益分配，高税率投资者获得的回报仅相当于7%的免税债券
> - 如果企业留存所有收益并持续增长，资本利得税后的净回报仍不如被动投资的税后回报
>
> 巴菲特用"绦虫"比喻通胀对企业资本的无情消耗——无论盈利多少，企业都必须不断投入更多资金到营运资本中。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> This was the most insightful section of the 1981 letter. Buffett argued that in a high interest rate environment (long-term bond yields exceeded 16% in 1981), a typical American business (with ~14% return on equity) was economically a "bad" business for individual investors:
> - If the company paid out all earnings, a high-tax investor would receive returns equivalent to only a 7% tax-exempt bond
> - If the company retained all earnings and grew consistently, the after-tax capital gains return was still worse than passive investment returns after tax
>
> Buffett used the "tapeworm" metaphor to describe inflation's relentless consumption of corporate capital—regardless of profitability, businesses had to continuously invest more in working capital.

### 保险行业状况 / Insurance Industry Conditions

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特预测1982年将是保险行业近年来最糟糕的承保年份。问题在于保险合同的期限特性：今年签下的保费合同约决定明年一半的收入水平。如果定价错误，保险公司不得不在相当长的时间内承受后果。
>
> 1981年行业综合比率达到105.7（超过100即意味着承保亏损），而保费增长率仅为3.6%。巴菲特指出，在没有重大自然灾害的情况下，行业保费收入需要至少10%的年增长才能稳住当前的承保亏损水平。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett predicted 1982 would be the worst underwriting year in recent history. The problem was the term nature of insurance contracts: this year's written premiums determined about half of next year's earned premiums. If pricing was wrong, insurers had to live with the consequences for a long time.
>
> The industry's 1981 combined ratio reached 105.7 (above 100 means underwriting losses), while premium growth was only 3.6%. Buffett noted that without major natural disasters, the industry would need at least 10% annual premium growth just to stabilize current underwriting loss levels.

### 股东指定捐赠 / Shareholder Designated Contributions

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特宣布了一项创新计划：允许股东指定伯克希尔慈善捐赠的接受者。在符合条件的932,206股中，95.6%的股东做出了回应，这一比例"即使排除巴菲特相关股份也超过90%"。总计1,783,655美元分配给了约675家慈善机构。这个想法来自查理·芒格。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett announced an innovative program allowing shareholders to designate recipients of Berkshire's charitable contributions. Of 932,206 eligible shares, 95.6% responded, a rate "exceeding 90% even excluding Buffett-related shares." A total of $1,783,655 was distributed to approximately 675 charities. The idea came from Charlie Munger.
