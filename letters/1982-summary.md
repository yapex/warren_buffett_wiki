## 年度财务表现概述 / Annual Financial Performance

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 1982年，伯克希尔·哈撒韦的经营收益为3,150万美元，仅相当于期初股东权益的9.8%（股权投资按成本计价），低于1981年的15.2%，更远低于1978年19.4%的近年高点。净资产增加2.08亿美元，以期初净资产5.19亿美元为基础，增长幅度为40%（按市值计算股权投资）。在现任管理层18年任期内，每股账面价值从19.46美元增长到737.43美元，年复合增长率为22.0%。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Operating earnings of $31.5 million in 1982 amounted to only 9.8% of beginning equity capital (valuing securities at cost), down from 15.2% in 1981 and far below our recent high of 19.4% in 1978. Net worth increased by $208 million, a 40% gain on a beginning net worth base of $519 million (valuing equity securities at market). During the 18-year tenure of present management, book value per share grew from $19.46 to $737.43, or 22.0% compounded annually.

### 保险承保业绩 / Insurance Underwriting Results

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 1982年保险承保出现严重亏损，税前承保损益为-2,155.8万美元，而1981年为147.8万美元的盈利。行业综合比率为109.5%（高于100表示亏损），远高于1981年的106.0%和1978年的97.5%。巴菲特指出，公司从盈利远高于行业平均水平滑落到略低于行业平均水平，主要原因是国民保险公司的传统险种定价已无法覆盖风险。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Insurance underwriting suffered a severe loss in 1982, with pre-tax underwriting results of -$21.558 million, compared to $1.478 million profit in 1981. The industry combined ratio was 109.5% (above 100 indicates a loss), far higher than 106.0% in 1981 and 97.5% in 1978. Buffett noted the company slipped from well above average profitability to slightly below industry average, primarily due to National Indemnity's traditional coverages being priced at levels guaranteeing underwriting losses.

### 净投资收益 / Net Investment Income

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 保险集团的净投资收益保持稳定增长，1982年为4,162万美元（1981年为3,882.3万美元），税后为3,527万美元（1981年为3,240.1万美元）。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Insurance Group net investment income remained steady and grew, reaching $41.62 million in 1982 (up from $38.823 million in 1981), or $35.27 million after-tax (up from $32.401 million).

## 核心投资理念 / Core Investment Philosophy

### 经济收益 vs 会计收益 / Economic vs. Accounting Earnings

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特在1982年信中深入阐述了"经济收益"与"会计收益"的区别。关键观点：
> - **会计局限性**：持股低于20%时，只有收到的现金股利计入收益，未分配收益被完全忽略
> - **GEICO案例**：1982年从GEICO收到的税后股利仅350万美元，但应享有的未分配经营收益达2,300万美元
> - **留存收益价值**：一家企业留存收益对股东的价值，取决于这些收益的运用效率，而非持股比例高低
> - **核心原则**："会计数字是企业估值的起点，而非终点"

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett elaborated on the distinction between "economic" and "accounting" earnings:
> - **Accounting limitations**: Below 20% ownership, only cash dividends are included in earnings; undistributed earnings are ignored
> - **GEICO example**: $3.5 million in after-tax dividends received from GEICO in 1982, but $23 million in share of undistributed operating earnings
> - **Retained earnings value**: The value of retained earnings to shareholders depends on how effectively those earnings are used, not ownership percentage
> - **Core principle**: "Accounting numbers are the beginning, not the end, of business valuation"

### 部分持股策略 / Partial Ownership Strategy

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特解释了他偏好的部分持股策略：
> - **投资优势**：在拍卖市场（股市）中，可以从几乎所有美国大型企业中挑选标的，许多公司质地远优于可通过协商整体收购的企业
> - **市场定价特点**：股市参与者行为模式"有时像一群时而狂躁、时而抑郁的旅鼠"，这为价值导向投资者创造了机会
> - **目标**：所投资企业的每一美元留存收益，最终能转化为至少一美元的市场价值
> - **"圣抵消"效应**：某些投资中留存收益对市场价值影响微乎其微甚至为负，但其他重要投资中，一美元留存可转化为两美元甚至更多市场价值

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett explained his partial ownership strategy:
> - **Investment advantages**: In the auction market (stock market), investors can select from almost the entire array of major American corporations, many far superior to businesses available for negotiated whole-company acquisition
> - **Market pricing characteristics**: Market participants "sometimes behave like an army of manic-depressive lemmings," creating opportunities for value-oriented investors
> - **Goal**: Each dollar of retained earnings from invested businesses translates into at least a dollar of market value
> - **"St. Offset" effect**: In some investments, retained earnings have insignificant or negative impact on market value, while in others, one dollar retained translates into two or more dollars of market value

### 留存收益的价值 / Value of Retained Earnings

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 1982年四大非控股公司（GEICO、General Foods、Washington Post、R.J. Reynolds）未分配经营收益中，伯克希尔应享有的份额远超4,000万美元
> - 这一数字完全未反映在报告收益中，仅列报了1,400万美元现金股利
> - 伯克希尔的长期目标是：实现远高于美国一般大型企业的长期回报率

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - In 1982, Berkshire's share of undistributed operating earnings from four major non-controlled holdings (GEICO, General Foods, Washington Post, R.J. Reynolds) came to well over $40 million
> - This number was not reflected at all in reported earnings; only $14 million in dividends received were reported
> - Berkshire's long-term goal: produce a long-term rate of return well above the return achieved by the average large American corporation

## 1982年关键事件 / Key Events in 1982

### 1. 一笔重大收购告吹 / A Major Acquisition Fell Through

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特坦承："你们的董事长去年也不安分地多次离开了那个房间，差点成了'1982年收购闹剧'的主角。回想起来，我们这一年最大的成就，竟然是一笔已经坚定承诺要做的巨额收购，因为完全不在我们控制范围内的原因而告吹了。如果这笔交易成了，它将消耗大量的时间和精力，换来的却是一个极不确定的结果。"

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett confessed: "Your Chairman left the room once too often last year and almost starred in the Acquisition Follies of 1982. In retrospect, our major accomplishment of the year was that a very large purchase to which we had firmly committed was unable to be completed for reasons totally beyond our control."

### 2. 保险业竞争格局变化 / Insurance Industry Competitive Changes

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特深入分析了保险业结构性变化：
> - **历史定价机制瓦解**：曾经由州监管机构推动的准管制定价体系已不再
> - **新竞争者涌入**：远超所需的新增产能迫使价格竞争成为主要武器
> - **客户觉醒**：消费者已认识到保险不再是"统一价"行业
> - **未来预期**：只有大幅缩小供需差距才能显著改善承保业绩，这需要"冲击性事件"（自然灾害或金融"超级灾难"）才能实现

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett deeply analyzed structural changes in the insurance industry:
> - **Historic pricing mechanism collapsed**: The quasi-administered pricing system fostered by state regulators is gone
> - **New competitors flooded in**: Excess new capacity forced price competition as the main weapon
> - **Customer awakening**: Consumers learned insurance is no longer a one-price business
> - **Future outlook**: Only a major narrowing of supply-demand gap can significantly improve underwriting results, requiring a "shock factor" (natural disaster or financial "megadisaster")

### 3. 管理层变动 / Management Changes

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> - 迈克·戈德伯格 ([Mike Goldberg](people/迈克·戈德伯格.md)) 接替巴菲特负责大部分保险业务的管理责任，在规划、招聘和监督方面带来显著改善
> - 菲尔·列舍（65岁）从国民保险公司退休
> - 本·罗斯纳（79岁）从联合零售退休

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> - Mike Goldberg replaced Buffett in responsibility for most insurance operations, bringing significant improvements in planning, recruitment, and monitoring
> - Phil Liesche (65) retired from National Indemnity Company
> - Ben Rosner (79) retired from Associated Retail Stores

### 4. 潜在的蓝筹印花合并 / Potential Blue Chip Merger

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 伯克希尔和蓝筹印花正在考虑于1983年进行合并，采用对两家公司适用同一估值方法的股票交换方式。这是伯克希尔或其关联公司在现任管理层任期内第二次重大发行新股（第一次是1978年与多元零售公司的合并）。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Berkshire and Blue Chip are considering merger in 1983, involving an exchange of stock based upon an identical valuation method applied to both companies. This would be the second significant share issuance during present management's tenure (the first was the 1978 merger with Diversified Retailing Company).

### 5. 发行股票的原则 / Principles on Share Issuance

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特详细阐述了避免价值稀释的股票发行原则：
> - **基本原则**：除非获得的内在商业价值与付出的一样多，否则不发行新股
> - **三种避免方法**：
>   1. 真正的以价值换价值的合并（如伯克希尔-蓝筹印花拟议合并）
> 2. 收购方股票以等于或高于内在商业价值的价格出售时
> 3. 先完成收购，再用回购修复价值损失
> - **警惕"双重打击"**：价值稀释的股票发行不仅直接损害内在商业价值，还会引发市场对管理层的不信任，导致股票长期偏低

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett elaborated principles to avoid value-diluting share issuance:
> - **Basic rule**: Issue shares only if receiving as much intrinsic business value as given
> - **Three ways to avoid**:
>   1. True business-value-for-business-value merger (like Berkshire-Blue Chip proposed merger)
>   2. When acquirer's stock sells at or above intrinsic business value
>   3. Complete acquisition, then repurchase to repair value damage
> - **Beware "double whammy"**: Value-diluting share issuances not only damage intrinsic business value directly but also trigger market distrust, causing long-term undervaluation

## 收购标准 / Acquisition Criteria

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 伯克希尔偏好的收购对象特征：
> 1. **规模要求**：税后净利润至少500万美元
> 2. **盈利稳定性**：具有持续稳定的盈利能力，不感兴趣"未来预测"和"困境反转"类项目
> 3. **财务稳健**：在很少使用或不使用负债的情况下，取得良好的股本回报率
> 4. **管理团队**：优秀的管理层已到位（伯克希尔无法提供管理层）
> 5. **业务简单**：业务简单，不涉及复杂技术
> 6. **明确报价**：有明确的报价，不想浪费双方时间讨论价格不确定的交易
>
> **交易偏好**：
> - 不参与恶意收购
> - 完全保密，快速答复（通常不超过五分钟）
> - 倾向现金交易，但如符合上述原则也考虑股票

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Berkshire's preferred acquisition characteristics:
> 1. **Size**: At least $5 million of after-tax earnings
> 2. **Consistent profitability**: Demonstrated consistent earning power; no interest in future projections or "turn-around" situations
> 3. **Financial strength**: Good returns on equity while employing little or no debt
> 4. **Management**: Quality management already in place (Berkshire cannot supply management)
> 5. **Business simplicity**: Simple businesses; won't understand complex technologies
> 6. **Clear pricing**: Clear offering price; won't waste time discussing transactions with unknown price
>
> **Transaction preferences**:
> - Will not engage in unfriendly transactions
> - Complete confidentiality, fast response (usually within five minutes)
> - Cash preferred, but will consider stock when consistent with principles above

## 关键洞见 / Key Insights

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 1982年信的核心洞见：
> 1. **衡量标准的局限性**：传统的"经营收益/股东权益"比率对伯克希尔的适用性已大不如前，因为非控股企业的未分配收益无法充分反映
> 2. **部分持股的优势**：在拍卖市场可以找到质地远优于整体收购的企业，且价格往往更具吸引力
> 3. **保险业结构性挑战**：监管套利消失后，保险业面临产能过剩与同质化产品的致命组合，短期难以改善
> 4. **价值导向投资**：强调长期"经济收益"而非短期"会计收益"，留存收益的运用效率比持股比例更重要
> 5. **管理层品质**：菲尔·列舍和本·罗斯纳的退休体现了"品格成就好运气"的管理哲学

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Key insights from the 1982 letter:
> 1. **Limitations of metrics**: Traditional "operating earnings/equity capital" ratio has diminished relevance for Berkshire due to undistributed earnings from non-controlled businesses
> 2. **Partial ownership advantages**: The auction market offers businesses far superior to those available for whole-company acquisition, often at more attractive prices
> 3. **Structural insurance challenges**: After regulatory arbitrage disappeared, insurance faces the deadly combination of over-capacity and commodity products, unlikely to improve short-term
> 4. **Value-oriented investing**: Emphasize long-term "economic earnings" over short-term "accounting earnings"; retained earnings efficiency matters more than ownership percentage
> 5. **Management quality**: Retirements of Phil Liesche and Ben Rosner embody the philosophy that "good character becomes good fortune"
