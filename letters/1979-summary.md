# 1979 年致股东信摘要 / 1979 Letter Summary

## 概览 / Overview

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 1979 年经营收益为期初股东权益（证券按成本计价）的 18.6%，低于 1978 年的 19.4%。每股收益增长约 20%，但巴菲特明确指出这是资金增加的自然结果而非管理绩效改善。信件首次提出"投资人痛苦指数"概念——通胀率加税负超过股本回报率时，投资人购买力必然缩水。股票投资组合市值从约 2.2 亿美元增至 3.37 亿美元。保险行业综合比率升至 100.7%，但伯克希尔自身降至 97.1%。巴菲特首次大篇幅讨论债券投资，抨击超长期固定利率债券在通胀时代的荒谬性。伊利诺伊国民银行将被迫剥离。伯克希尔开始在纳斯达克交易。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> 1979 operating earnings were 18.6% of beginning shareholders' equity (securities at cost), down from 19.4% in 1978. Per-share earnings rose about 20%, but Buffett explicitly warned this reflects capital growth rather than improved management performance. The letter introduced the "investor's misery index" — when inflation plus taxes exceeds return on equity, purchasing power shrinks. The stock portfolio grew from approximately $220M to $337M in market value. Industry combined ratio rose to 100.7%, but Berkshire's own improved to 97.1%. Buffett devoted significant space to criticizing long-term fixed-rate bonds in an inflationary era. Illinois National Bank must be divested. Berkshire began NASDAQ trading.

## 核心主题 / Key Themes

1. **每股收益的误导性** — 资金增加自然推高 EPS，"停摆的钟"也能像成长股
2. **投资人痛苦指数** — 通胀率 + 税负 > 股本回报率 = 购买力缩水，企业对此束手无策
3. **黄金与石油的类比** — 1964 年至今每股账面价值仍只能换半盎司黄金
4. **困境反转很少反转** — Waumbec 纺织厂收购是个错误，好公司合理价 > 烂公司便宜价
5. **保险周期恶化** — 行业综合比率升至 100.7%，预计未来五年平均 105
6. **超长期债券的荒谬** — 一年期保单太短却敢借出三四十年，"既不做短期借款人，也不做长期放贷人"
7. **股东群体如同餐厅顾客** — 菲利普·费雪的比喻：公司吸引它所追求也配得上的股东
8. **伊利诺伊国民银行将剥离** — 美联储拒绝分拆方案，可能出售 80%-100% 股权

## 精选原文 / Notable Excerpts

> [!quote] 每股收益的误导性 / EPS Misleading
> 即便是一家"停摆的钟"，只要分红率够低，看起来也像一只成长股。判断一家公司经营管理层绩效好坏的首要标准，应该是看它在不过度使用杠杆、不玩会计把戏的前提下，能否取得高水平的股本回报率，而不是看每股收益是否持续增长。
>
> Even a "stopped clock" can look like a growth stock if the dividend payout ratio is low. The primary test of managerial economic performance is the achievement of a high earnings rate on equity capital employed (without undue leverage, accounting gimmickry, etc.) and not the achievement of consistent gains in earnings per share.

> [!quote] 投资人痛苦指数 / Investor's Misery Index
> 通货膨胀率加上股东将公司年度收益收入囊中时必须缴纳的资本比例——这两者合起来可以称作"投资人痛苦指数"。当这个指数超过企业的股本回报率时，投资人的购买力就会缩水，即便他什么都没有消费。对此我们束手无策，因为高通胀率并不会帮我们赚取更高的股本回报率。
>
> That combination — the inflation rate plus the percentage of capital that must be paid by the owner to transfer into his own pocket the annual earnings achieved by the business — can be thought of as an "investor's misery index". When this index exceeds the rate of return earned on equity by the business, the investor's purchasing power shrinks even though he consumes nothing at all. We have no corporate solution to this problem.

> [!quote] 困境反转很少反转 / Turnarounds Seldom Turn
> 无论是经营还是投资的经验都让我们得出一个结论：所谓的"困境反转"很少真的能反转，同样的精力和才华，花在以合理价格买入好公司上，远比花在以便宜价格买入烂公司上要值得。
>
> Both our operating and investment experience cause us to conclude that "turnarounds" seldom turn, and that the same energies and talent are much better employed in a good business purchased at a fair price than in a poor business purchased at a bargain price.

> [!quote] 超长期债券的荒谬 / Absurdity of Long-term Bonds
> 许多人一边认为在通胀环境下一年的保单定价期限都太长了，转过身来就把卖掉半年期保单收到的保费，以固定价格借出去三四十年。
>
> Having decided that one year is too long a period for which to set a fixed price for insurance in an inflationary world, they then have turned around, taken the proceeds from the sale of that six-month policy, and sold the money at a fixed price for thirty or forty years.

> [!quote] 股东如同餐厅顾客 / Shareholders as Restaurant Patrons
> 一家公司吸引股东的策略比作一家餐厅吸引顾客。一家餐厅可以锁定特定的客户群并培养出一群忠实的回头客。但一家餐厅不能三天两头换风格，却指望拥有一批满意而稳定的顾客。
>
> The policies of the corporation in attracting shareholders were likened to those of a restaurant attracting potential customers. A restaurant could seek a given clientele and eventually obtain an appropriate group of devotees. But the restaurant could not change its character constantly and end up with a happy and stable clientele.

> [!quote] 集中化财务决策与极度分权 / Centralized Finance, Extreme Delegation
> 财务决策集中于最高层，同时将运营权力极大程度地下放给各子公司或业务单元的关键经理人。我们总部的团队人数刚好能凑出一支篮球队。最重要的是，它使我们能够吸引并留住一些极为优秀的人才——因为在伯克希尔工作对他们来说几乎等同于自己当老板。
>
> Your company is run on the principle of centralization of financial decisions at the top, and rather extreme delegation of operating authority to a number of key managers. We could just field a basketball team with our corporate headquarters group. Most important of all, it enables us to attract and retain some extraordinarily talented individuals — who find working for Berkshire to be almost identical to running their own show.
