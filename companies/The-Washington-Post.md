# The Washington Post / 华盛顿邮报

## 公司简介 / Company Profile

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 华盛顿邮报公司 (The Washington Post Company) 是美国主要的新闻媒体集团，旗下拥有《华盛顿邮报》报纸、电视台和杂志等媒体资产。1982年，伯克希尔是其第二大股东。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> The Washington Post Company is a major American media group, owning the Washington Post newspaper, television stations, and magazines. In 1982, Berkshire was its second largest shareholder.

## 伯克希尔的投资 / Berkshire's Investment

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 1982年，伯克希尔持有华盛顿邮报 1,868,600 股，成本仅为1,062.8万美元，市值高达1.0324亿美元——这是伯克希尔最大的未实现收益来源之一。巴菲特早在13岁时就与华盛顿邮报建立了商业联系。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> In 1982, Berkshire held 1,868,600 shares of Washington Post at a cost of only $10.628 million with a market value of $103.24 million - one of Berkshire's largest sources of unrealized gains. Buffett first connected with Washington Post at age 13.

## 投资教训 / Investment Lesson

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 巴菲特从华盛顿邮报的投资中学到了重要一课："选股时，念旧情绪必须给予足够的重视。"他与华盛顿邮报的渊源可以追溯到13岁，与GEICO的渊源始于20岁。虽然在1970年代中期才以投资者身份回归，但这一"姗姗来迟的忠诚"带来了丰厚的回报。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Buffett learned an important investment lesson from Washington Post: "Nostalgia should be weighted heavily in stock selection." His connection with Washington Post dates back to age 13, and with GEICO to age 20. Although he only returned as an investor in the mid-1970s, this "long-delayed corporate fidelity" was richly rewarded.

## 竞争优势 / Competitive Position

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> 华盛顿邮报代表了伯克希尔四大非控股投资之一。根据1982年的数据，其未分配经营收益中伯克希尔应享有的份额远超4,000万美元。这体现了伯克希尔"经济收益"概念的重要性——留存收益对全体股东的价值，取决于这些收益的运用效率，而不是持股比例的大小。

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> Washington Post represents one of Berkshire's four major non-controlled investments. According to 1982 data, Berkshire's share of undistributed operating earnings exceeded $40 million. This illustrates Berkshire's concept of "economic" earnings - the value to all owners of retained earnings is determined by the effectiveness with which those earnings are used, not by the size of one's ownership percentage.

## 巴菲特原话 / Buffett Quotes

> [!zh] 🇨🇳
> "如果你还没注意到的话，这张表有个重要的投资经验值得分享：选股时，念旧情绪必须给予足够的重视。我们最大的两笔未实现收益来自华盛顿邮报和GEICO——你们的董事长分别在13岁和20岁时就与这两家公司初结商缘。在迷失了大约25年之后，我们在1970年代中期才以投资者的身份重新归来。这张表充分量化了这份'姗姗来迟的忠诚'所带来的回报。"

> [!en] 🇺🇸
> "In case you haven't noticed, there is an important investment lesson to be derived from this table: nostalgia should be weighted heavily in stock selection. Our two largest unrealized gains are in Washington Post and GEICO, companies with which your Chairman formed his first commercial connections at the ages of 13 and 20, respectively. After straying for roughly 25 years, we returned as investors in the mid-1970s. The table quantifies the rewards for even long-delayed corporate fidelity."
